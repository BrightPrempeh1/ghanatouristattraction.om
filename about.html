About Ghana

Ghana is a country located on the West African coast and is known for its rich history, cultural diversity, and peaceful environment. It was the first African country south of the Sahara to gain independence in 1957.

The country is home to many ethnic groups, each with unique traditions, festivals, music, and clothing. Ghanaian hospitality is famous worldwide, making visitors feel welcomed and safe.

Ghana also plays an important role in African and world history, especially during the transatlantic slave trade era. Today, it stands as a symbol of freedom, resilience, and cultural pride.

Location

Ghana is bordered by Côte d’Ivoire, Burkina Faso, Togo, and the Atlantic Ocean.

People & Culture

Over 75 ethnic groups with rich traditions, languages, and festivals.

Peace & Stability

One of the most peaceful and stable countries in West Africa.
